## Summary Statistics

### Multi-Concept Operators
**Count:** 8 groups
**Total Locations:** ~50+ locations
**Average Concepts per Group:** 5.6

**Characteristics:**
- More complex operations
- Different scheduling needs per concept
- Often mix QSR, casual, fine dining, nightlife
- Higher operational sophistication required
- Need system that handles multiple brands under one corporate umbrella

### Single-Brand Operators
**Count:** 5 groups
**Total Locations:** ~48+ locations
**Average Locations per Brand:** 9.6

**Characteristics:**
- Standardized operations
- Consistent scheduling patterns across locations
- Easier to scale workforce management
- Often seeking efficiency across many similar locations
- Need system that handles multi-location management

## Sales Strategy Implications

### For Multi-Concept Operators:

**Value Proposition:**
- "Manage different restaurant concepts from one system"
- "Different scheduling templates for fine dining vs. casual vs. nightlife"
- "Corporate-level visibility across all brands"
- "Custom labor models for each concept"

**Key Features to Highlight:**
- Multi-location groups with different brands
- Role-based permissions (by concept/location)
- Customizable scheduling templates
- Consolidated reporting across concepts

**Complexity = Higher Value:**
- More pain points = more value from solution
- Willing to pay more for sophisticated features
- Often have IT/operations teams to work with

**Top Priorities:**
1. **Ariete Hospitality Group** - 10+ concepts, Michelin-quality, sophisticated
2. **Groot Hospitality** - High-volume, well-capitalized, complex mix
3. **Fire Pit Hospitality** - Growing, different formats

---

### For Single-Brand Operators:

**Value Proposition:**
- "Scale your proven concept efficiently across all locations"
- "Standardize scheduling across 30+ locations"
- "Replicate your best practices everywhere"
- "Corporate oversight with local control"

**Key Features to Highlight:**
- Template replication across locations
- Cross-location shift coverage
- Bulk schedule publishing
- Multi-location analytics

**Scale = Higher Volume:**
- More locations = more seats = more revenue potential
- Often price-sensitive (need to work across many locations)
- May have less sophistication but more volume

**Top Priorities:**
1. **Sushi Sake Corporate** - 30+ locations, largest scale opportunity
2. **Pura Vida Miami** - 12+ locations, multi-market expansion
3. **Chifa Du Kang** - 3 locations, growing regional chain

## Key Insight: Different Buying Patterns

### Multi-Concept Operators:
- **Buy for:** Complexity management, corporate control
- **Pain point:** "How do we manage 10 different restaurant brands?"
- **Decision maker:** Corporate COO/CFO
- **Sales cycle:** Longer (more stakeholders)
- **Price sensitivity:** Lower (solving complex problem)

### Single-Brand Operators:
- **Buy for:** Efficiency, standardization, scale
- **Pain point:** "How do we replicate best practices across 30 locations?"
- **Decision maker:** VP Operations / Regional Manager
- **Sales cycle:** Medium (fewer stakeholders but more locations)
- **Price sensitivity:** Higher (cost per location matters)
